## 8. Story Bank

### Story 1: TORIC Platform — "The Operating Model Transformer"
**Best for:** Problem discovery, user empathy, adoption, change management, user-centered design
**Format:** HPARL (Hook, Principles, Action, Results, Learnings)

**Hook:** "Executives were making risk decisions by squinting at spreadsheets on their phones."

**Principles:**
- Technology should fix the root of a broken process, not just automate the broken steps
- Know your audience — listen, understand, clarify pain points for each persona
- Any change is 70% people and 30% tech

**Action:**
- Inherited a failed risk reporting pilot. Executives resisted because the tool surfaced issues they'd have to defend.
- Conducted persona-based discovery sessions: process mapping, identified key pain points per persona, defined north star metrics
- First attempt was a bespoke automation pilot — learned it wasn't repeatable. This is where the self-serve BI portal idea was born
- Redesigned for transparency: risk thresholds visible to everyone, ops analysts could provide commentary against flagged items, ownership assignment became self-serve
- Did persona-based training sessions anchored on WIIFM ("what's in it for you")
- Shipped MVP with a two-person team on $50K budget. Validated with early adopter champions before scaling.

**Results:**
- Cut executive reporting requests by 60%
- Grew to 250+ active users across risk, compliance, and operations
- Freed ~$200K/year (~0.5 FTE per business line)
- BAU support requests dropped 50%
- Governance meetings transformed from defensive debates into strategic conversations about risk management

**Learnings:** "The technical solution was only 30% of the battle. The other 70% was about changing the culture and workflow."

**Key product details for deep-dive questions:**
- North star metric: KRI (Key Risk Indicators) breaching governance thresholds
- Dashboard design driven by insight: executives only cared about what's breaking (RAG status, red items)
- Designed for actual user behavior (scanning for risk on phones between meetings), not theoretical use case
- Feature vs outcome: the dashboard was the feature; executives seeing what's breaking without asking for a report was the outcome
- Failure modes: data accuracy from org changes, product clutter from scope creep, loss of executive sponsorship
- Prioritization: chose pilot group based on stakeholder willingness to champion (not loudest voice). Generic self-serve platform over bespoke customization.

**Reuse when asked about:**
- "Tell me about a time you identified a problem users couldn't articulate"
- Driving adoption / change management / overcoming resistance
- Data-driven product decisions (north star metric insight)

## 12. Interview Coaching Lessons

### Three Laws Framework
1. **Law #1: How you say it matters more than what you say.** Confidence, energy, conviction create a halo effect. Hedging language ("I think," "kind of," "maybe") triggers skepticism.
2. **Law #2: Flip your weakness into a strength.** Don't wait for them to notice gaps. Address head-on and reframe as evidence of capability.
3. **Law #3: Don't volunteer information that can hurt you.** Every sentence either builds your case or undermines it. No neutral ground.

### Delivery Rules
- 90 seconds max per answer in interviews. Founders have short attention spans.
- Lead with outcome, not setup. Start with the result or decision, then fill in the how.
- When you don't know, say so, then bridge. "I haven't worked in X directly. Here's how I've learned comparable complexity before."
- Slow down at metrics. Pause before and after the number. Let it land.
- Don't memorize word-for-word. Know the blocks, then talk naturally.
